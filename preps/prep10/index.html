Weekly Preps

Each week, you’ll complete a prep exercise to learn new material and prepare for lecture. Each prep is divided into a series of short-answer comprehension questions hosted in a Quercus quiz, and then a series of programming exercises to be submitted on MarkUs.

General instructions

  1. You must submit all prep work individually. However, you may freely discuss all of your answers with your classmates, and with TAs and instructors on the course discussion board and office hours.
  2. For all parts of the prep hosted on Quercus, you can make as many attempts as you wish. Your score is shown after each attempt. If you submit multiple attempts, only your best score is used.
  3. For the Synthesize (programming) portion of the prep, you may submit the required file(s) as many times as you want on MarkUs. However, your submission is only graded after the deadline has passed. Only your most recent submission before the deadline will be graded.
  4. The prep deadline is firm, and no late submissions (either on Quercus or MarkUs) are accepted. You may not use grace tokens to extend this deadline.

Grading

Your submission for each prep is graded automatically, with the following division of marks:

  • Comprehension questions (50%): this is your score on the questions on Quercus.
  • Synthesize—correctness (40%): this is based on the percentage of tests passed from our automated testing suite. We will run additional tests beyond the sample ones provided, so it is your responsibility to create your own tests to verify the correctness of your submission.
  • Synthesize—PythonTA (10%): this is based on the number of errors or warnings reported by running python_ta.check_all on your code. Every error results in a 1% deduction (up to a maximum of 10% total). Multiple errors of the same type are counted individually.
